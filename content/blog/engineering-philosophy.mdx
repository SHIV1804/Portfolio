## The Hype Cycle

The software industry moves at a breakneck pace, with new frameworks and paradigms appearing weekly. While innovation is necessary, it often comes at the cost of maintainability and long-term stability.

## The Power of 'Boring'

"Boring" technology is technology that is well-understood, well-documented, and has a proven track record. For a systems engineer, boring is beautiful. It means fewer surprises in production and a clearer path to resolution when things inevitably go wrong.

### My Core Principles

1. **Solve the Problem, Not the Tool**: Don't add a dependency unless the problem it solves is greater than the complexity it introduces.
2. **Readability over Cleverness**: Code is read much more often than it is written.
3. **Understand the Layers Below**: Even if you work in a high-level language, knowing how the OS and hardware operate will make you a better developer.

## Final Thoughts

Building software that lasts requires a disciplined focus on the fundamentals. By embracing minimalism and valuing stability, we create systems that serve their users reliably for years to come.
